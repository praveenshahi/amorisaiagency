A Recruiting Agent screens every application against your job description, scores and ranks candidates, sends first-round questions to shortlisted applicants, and books interview slots in your calendar — so your HR team spends time on final-round decisions, not initial screening. Starting at ₹1.5L setup + ₹20k/month.

## What it does

Reviewing 200 resumes for a single role takes 8–10 hours. Most of that is disqualification — 80% of applicants don't meet basic criteria. This agent does the disqualification work so your team sees the 20% worth their time.

- **Resume screening:** Every application read against your JD and criteria, scored 1–100
- **Shortlist generation:** Top candidates identified with a 3-line summary of why they fit
- **Screening questions sent:** Shortlisted candidates get personalised first-round questions automatically
- **Answer evaluation:** Candidate responses scored against ideal answers
- **Interview scheduling:** Qualified candidates book their own slot from your calendar link
- **Rejection handling:** Non-shortlisted candidates receive a professional decline email

## How it works

**Step 1 — JD and criteria defined.** You share the job description and scoring criteria. The agent builds the evaluation rubric.

**Step 2 — Applications arrive.** Via email, Naukri, LinkedIn, or your careers page. Agent processes each one.

**Step 3 — Screening and ranking.** Agent reads each resume, scores it, adds a shortlist note: "7 years Python, led 3-person team, worked at fintech companies, missing: cloud experience."

**Step 4 — Shortlisted candidates contacted.** Personalised email: "Thanks for applying to [role] at [Company]. We'd like to learn more. Can you answer 3 quick questions?"

**Step 5 — HR team reviews the top 10%.** Summaries with resume, screening answers, and agent score. No raw resume pile. Interview slots already scheduled for final candidates.
